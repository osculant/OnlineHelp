Frequently Asked Questions

A computer virus is a type of malicious software (malware) that attaches itself to legitimate programs or files. Once executed, it can replicate, spread to other files, and perform unauthorized actions like stealing data, damaging your system, or slowing down performance.

Modern cyber threats are sophisticated and constant. Antivirus software acts as a gatekeeper, providing real-time protection, blocking malicious downloads, preventing access to scam websites, and scanning your files to stop malware before it can cause damage.

AVG AntiVirus Free is trusted by millions because it offers award-winning, industrial-strength protection without the price tag. It provides real-time updates, advanced behavioral analysis to catch new threats, and a very light system impact, meaning it won't slow down your laptop.

AVG uses "Core Shields." These monitor your files in real-time, scan incoming emails for malicious attachments, block dangerous website scripts, and analyze the behavior of programs on your computer to identify and stop suspicious activity, even if it hasn't been seen before.

It's simple: download and install your chosen free antivirus software. Once installed, open the dashboard and click "Smart Scan" or "Run Scan." The software will automatically check your system files, background processes, and connected drives for any threats.

If you suspect an infection, disconnect from the internet immediately to prevent the virus from spreading. Run a "Full System Scan" using your antivirus software. If it finds a threat, the software will automatically isolate (quarantine) or remove the malicious files for you.
